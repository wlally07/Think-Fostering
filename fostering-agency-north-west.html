Why Foster in the North West of England?

The North West of England encompasses a diverse range of communities, from the vibrant cities of Manchester and Liverpool to the rural landscapes of Lancashire, Cumbria and the Lake District. Each area has its own unique fostering needs, and local agencies work closely with councils across Greater Manchester, Merseyside, Lancashire, Cheshire and Cumbria to place children in homes where they can thrive.

Fostering in the North West offers the opportunity to make a genuine difference to children who need stability, security and care. Independent fostering agencies in the region provide specialist support including therapeutic fostering, emergency placements, sibling group care, and dedicated training programmes designed to equip foster carers with the skills and confidence they need. Many agencies in the North West are rated Outstanding or Good by Ofsted, reflecting the high standard of care and professional support available to foster families across the region.

Whether you live in a city centre flat in Manchester or a countryside home in the Ribble Valley, there is a fostering agency that can support your journey. Browse the agencies below, compare what they offer, and get in touch with those that feel right for you. Every enquiry is free, confidential, and without obligation.

Fostering Across the North West

Explore fostering opportunities in each area of the North West

Fostering in Manchester & Greater Manchester

Manchester is the largest city in the North West and home to a significant number of children who need foster care placements. Greater Manchester encompasses ten local authority areas including Salford, Stockport, Tameside, Oldham, Rochdale, Bury, Bolton, Wigan, Trafford and the city of Manchester itself. Each area has its own fostering needs, and independent agencies work closely with all ten councils to provide placements.

Foster carers in Manchester benefit from access to excellent training facilities, diverse placement opportunities, and strong local support networks. The city's cultural diversity means agencies actively seek carers from a wide range of backgrounds to provide culturally matched placements for children in care.

Fostering in Liverpool & Merseyside

Liverpool and the wider Merseyside region, including Wirral, Sefton, Knowsley and St Helens, have a strong tradition of community and family values that make the area particularly welcoming for foster carers. Merseyside has consistently high demand for foster placements, particularly for teenagers, sibling groups, and children with additional needs.

Fostering agencies in Liverpool offer dedicated support teams, regular peer group meetings, and access to specialist therapeutic services. Many agencies in the Merseyside area maintain Ofsted ratings of Good or Outstanding, reflecting the commitment to high-quality care and professional development for foster carers.

Find agencies in Liverpool →

Fostering in Lancashire

Lancashire is one of the largest counties in England, stretching from the industrial towns of Blackburn, Burnley and Preston to the seaside resorts of Blackpool and Morecambe, and the rural beauty of the Ribble Valley and Forest of Bowland. The county has a diverse and growing need for foster carers across all placement types.

Independent fostering agencies operating across Lancashire provide comprehensive support packages including regular home visits, accredited training programmes, and 24-hour out-of-hours support. Foster carers in Lancashire benefit from a strong sense of community, and many agencies organise social events and activity days for foster families throughout the year.

Find agencies in Lancashire →

Fostering in Cheshire

Cheshire, encompassing Cheshire East, Cheshire West and Chester, Warrington and Halton, is a county that blends historic market towns with modern communities. The area has a steady demand for foster carers, particularly for short-term and respite placements, as well as long-term foster homes for older children and teenagers.

Agencies based in Cheshire are known for providing personalised, one-to-one support and maintaining smaller caseloads so that every foster carer receives the attention and guidance they deserve. The county's excellent schools and safe communities make it an appealing location for foster families looking to offer children stability and opportunity.

Find agencies in Cheshire →

Fostering in Cumbria & the Lake District

Cumbria, home to the Lake District National Park, Carlisle, Barrow-in-Furness and Kendal, is a unique fostering environment that combines rural living with close-knit communities. Children placed in Cumbria often benefit from the outdoor lifestyle, smaller school settings, and strong community support that characterise the region.

Fostering agencies in Cumbria understand the particular challenges of rural placements, including travel distances and access to services, and provide tailored support to address these. There is a particular need for foster carers in Cumbria who can offer stable, long-term homes, and agencies in the area actively recruit carers who value community, nature and a slower pace of life.

Commonly Asked Questions About Fostering in the North West

Thinking about fostering is a big step, and it is completely natural to have questions about eligibility, support, payments, training and what everyday life as a foster carer involves; the information below covers the most common topics to help you feel informed and confident, and if you would like to know more, please read our full FAQs or contact a member of the team using our contact form.

The North West of England is served by over 45 Ofsted-registered fostering agencies, including both independent fostering agencies and local authority fostering services. These agencies operate across Greater Manchester, Merseyside, Lancashire, Cheshire and Cumbria, each offering different specialisms, support structures and allowance packages.

Think Fostering helps you compare these agencies in one place, so you can find the right fit for your circumstances without needing to research each one individually.

  • Over 45 registered agencies across the region
  • Both independent and local authority services available
  • Compare agencies free of charge on Think Fostering

Fostering allowances in the North West vary between agencies and depend on the age of the child, the type of placement and whether additional skills-based fees are included. As a general guide, weekly allowances typically range from around £400 to £600 or more per week, with higher rates for specialist or therapeutic placements.

Many independent fostering agencies in the North West offer additional payments for birthdays, holidays, clothing and activities. It is important to compare the full financial package, not just the headline allowance, when choosing an agency.

  • Allowances typically range from £400 to £600+ per week
  • Additional payments for birthdays and holidays common
  • Compare full packages, not just headline rates

No prior fostering experience is required. All fostering agencies in the North West provide comprehensive training before you are approved as a foster carer, covering child development, safeguarding, managing challenging behaviour, and the legal framework around looked-after children. Many agencies also offer ongoing professional development after approval.

What agencies look for is personal commitment, resilience, empathy and a genuine desire to provide a safe and supportive home for children. Life experience, whether as a parent, teacher, youth worker or simply a caring individual, is valued alongside formal qualifications.

  • No prior experience needed to become a foster carer
  • Full training provided before and after approval
  • Personal qualities valued alongside qualifications

Choosing the right fostering agency is a personal decision that depends on your circumstances, preferences and expectations. Start by reviewing Ofsted inspection reports for agencies in your area, which are publicly available and provide independent assessments of safeguarding, leadership and carer support quality.

Compare what each agency offers in terms of training, supervision frequency, financial allowances and out-of-hours support. Speaking directly to agencies and, where possible, to existing foster carers will give you the clearest picture of what day-to-day fostering life is really like with that provider.

  • Review Ofsted reports for independent assessments
  • Compare training, support and allowance packages
  • Speak directly to agencies and existing carers
